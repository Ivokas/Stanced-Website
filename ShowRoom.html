Stance

In the world of automotive culture, stance isn't just a style—it's a philosophy, a craft, and an art form. It allows a car to reflect its owner's creativity and personality, pushing boundaries and achieving perfection in proportions. Stance is about creating a vehicle that exudes presence, whether parked or rolling. But what exactly is stance, and what makes building a stance car so unique? At its core, stance refers to the way a car "sits" or presents itself. It involves the suspension setup, wheel fitment, ride height, and overall posture. A properly stanced car features a lowered suspension, carefully chosen wheel offsets, stretched tires, and flush or aggressive camber angles for a bold, cohesive look. While aesthetics are key, stance is also about harmonizing these elements to achieve visual perfection.

Stance essencials

  • Suspension: The foundation of any stanced car, involving adjustments like coilovers, air suspension, or static drops to achieve the desired ride height and handling.
  • Bodywork: Enhancements such as widebody kits, fender rolling, and custom bumpers create space for extreme fitment and refine the car's proportions.
  • Wheel Fitment: The heart of stance culture, combining perfect wheel offset, tire stretching, and camber angles to achieve a flush or aggressive look.
  • Aesthetics: Final touches include bold paint, custom wheels, lighting accents, and personalized interiors that reflect the owner's style and complete the car's visual appeal.
